• Martin Grim

    Section Head Electronics Engineering

    The Engineering Group (EG) at SRON designs and builds advanced research and aerospace instruments in collaboration with scientists and external partners. The team consists of about 55–60 engineers and supports nearly all SRON projects, including satellite, aircraft, and balloon-based instruments, while investing in new technologies such as cubesat engineering and 3D printing. The group is divided into four disciplines—Electronic Design, Mechanical Design, Product & Quality Assurance, and Engineering Realization—and focuses on project realization, strategic development, leadership, and collaboration with industry and research institutes.

  • Benjamin Ricketts

    PhD candidate

    I work on numerical models for X-ray astronomy, focusing on the reltrans model that simulates X-ray emission near black holes. To handle its complexity, I use machine learning to emulate model outputs, speeding up data analysis and improving the code. I also serve as Principal Investigator for the NEBULA-Xplorer mission at SRON, an educational satellite project where students design and build a real mission to monitor X-ray binary systems and study phenomena such as jet ejections and accretion processes.
